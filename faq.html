Frequently Asked Questions


How does the scheduler optimise the schedules?

The scheduler optimises the NPV based on costs and revenues whist obeying all specified constraints. In addition to operating costs, capital expenditure can also be specified. Unlike heuristic schedulers, maximum NPV for the scenario is guaranteed as an exact mathematical model is solved to generate the schedule.

Can you use block by block data?

Yes, the block by block data can be imported into the software. While scheduling the scheduler will mine bench by bench, and if the bench proportions are ticked on, then it will mine the blocks proportionately.

The scheduler works on bench by bench basis. This that correct?

Yes, mining of a pit happens sequentially bench by bench. Although bench by bench mining occurs, there are blocks or parcels of data that are being preserved and separately mined. When we say bench by bench mining occurs, it means that there are no block or parcel dependencies on a bench. As GNOS is a strategic scheduler and the resolution is generally on annual periods, block dependencies are not important.

What is the largest model size that can be handled?

There is no restriction on the size of the model although there are practical considerations on solve realistic solve times. Larger models can take from hours to days to solve and perhaps don’t produce any better results than a more streamlined model with much faster and practical run times. Even in today’s advanced technology and computers, these problems can become very large to solve and that is why modelling approach becomes important when solving larger models.

What is care and maintenance cost and how is this important?

In an optimized scenario, there are cases where one or multiple pits being suspended after partial mining for few years makes a better financial prospect. But in real world more often than not, we have to incur some operating cost to maintain the pits in operational condition so that we can recover the ore when required. In some condition this additional cost has a potential to alter the outcome of the schedule result. Care and Maintenance feature in GNOS gives user to capture this practical aspect of mining while running their scenario. It lets the user to understand that after incurring this additional cost, does suspending the pit still makes a better economic scenario. Using this feature is up to the user discretion.

Why is closure considered in scheduling and what impact does it have?

Many mines have statutory requirement of backfilling the pits once mining is complete. This is more often the case when mining occurs below water table and upon completion of mining, the pits have to be backfilled to pre-mining water table RL. GNOS provides an integrated way of backfilling the pits during mining and or reclaiming the dumps when mining is complete. This integration of closure planning not only addresses the backfill obligations, but also provides the options with least cost or best NPV.

What is the difference between the stockpile reclaim in global and sliding mode runs?

Unlike other optimized scheduling software, GNOS provides unique options of reclaiming both in global or sliding window mode. However, there is a difference in the concept of reclaim in global and sliding window mode. In Global mode, the stockpiling is considered as a paddock dump, and every paddock is available to be reclaimed whenever required. Whereas in Sliding window scenario, all materials going to a single stockpile is aggregated and the aggregated inventory is available for reclaim.
